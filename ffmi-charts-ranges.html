FFMI Ranges and Performance Categories

Compare your FFMI score against established benchmarks to interpret your physique assessment and set new training goals.

FFMI Performance Table (Men)

The FFMI score provides a general guideline for muscle mass relative to height. Use the table below to determine your category.

FFMI Score Category Implication
< 17.0 Below Average Indicates low muscle mass relative to height.
17.0 – 19.0 Average Typical for the general, untrained population.
19.0 – 21.0 Above Average Achievable with consistent training and good nutrition.
21.0 – 23.0 Excellent (Trained) Represents a high level of muscularity, common among dedicated, natural athletes.
> 23.0 Exceptional (Advanced/Elite) Often cited as the upper limit for natural potential. May indicate advanced genetics or long-term training dedication.

Note on FFMI for Women

While the underlying formula is the same, FFMI ranges for women tend to be slightly lower than for men due to biological differences in body composition. A score of 18.0 or above is typically considered **Excellent** for a woman.
