Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Find quick answers about the Fat-Free Mass Index, its calculation, accuracy, and interpretation.

**BMI (Body Mass Index)** uses total body weight and height, making it unable to distinguish between fat and muscle. FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index) uses **Lean Body Mass (LBM)**, which excludes body fat, providing a much better indicator of muscularity relative to height. If you are very muscular, your FFMI will be high, whereas your BMI might inaccurately label you as overweight or obese.

The FFMI formula itself is mathematically precise. However, the final accuracy of your score is entirely dependent on the accuracy of the input **Body Fat Percentage (BF%)**. We recommend using the most reliable BF% methods available (like DEXA scans or hydrostatic weighing) rather than simple bioimpedance scales or estimates for the most meaningful result.

The normalization allows for a more equitable comparison of muscularity potential between people of different heights.
